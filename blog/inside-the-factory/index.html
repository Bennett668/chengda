01 Where it's made

Why Chaozhou Is China's Ceramic Sanitaryware Capital

Chaozhou, in eastern Guangdong, is the largest ceramic sanitaryware production base in China. Generations of kilns, glaze chemists and mould-makers sit within a few kilometres of each other. It is the reason a Chengda basin can move from a sketch to a fired sample in weeks, not months. Today, thousands of ceramic wash basins, toilets and bathroom products are produced here every day for customers around the world.

We have manufactured here since 1999. Not a sales office, not a middleman — the people who answer your email work in the same building as the kiln.

The clearest answer to "who am I really buying from?" is to show you the floor.

02 The process

How Ceramic Wash Basins and Toilets Are Manufactured

Vitreous china is unforgiving — every stage has to hold, or the basin fails years later in a customer's bathroom. Here is the line at a glance.

Five stages, one roof

StageWhat happensWhy it matters
CastingCeramic wash basins and toilets begin as liquid slip poured into plaster moulds; the body forms as the mould draws out water.Decides wall thickness and shape accuracy.
FiringHigh-temperature firing at 1,250 °C strengthens the ceramic body through a full kiln cycle.Vitrifies the body — under 0.5% water absorption.
GlazingThe glaze gives every wash basin and toilet a sealed, gloss or matte surface, sprayed and re-fired.Stain resistance and the finish the buyer sees.
QCEvery sanitaryware product is inspected — flush testing, crazing and dimension checks, visual grading.Catches defects before they ship.
PackingExport packing protects every ceramic basin and toilet — foam, cartons and pallets, retail or OEM-branded.Survives the sea freight to 50+ countries.
